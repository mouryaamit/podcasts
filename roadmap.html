As the Index increases its coverage it will offer its readers, increasingly granular insights into the MSME economy – by industry type, core business activity and by geographic cluster.

The consolidated Jocata Sumpoorn Index is a starting point. The depth of data on the MSME business fetched from GST, lends a tremendous opportunity to deconstruct the Index into constituent parts.

The potential of the data is phenomenal and our efforts to launch these sub-index offerings to the community is already in the works.

Jocata Sumpoorn – Industry-wise Sub-Index

The industry-wise sub-index will offer insights into how different industries, as classified under GST by the Harmonized System of Nomenclature (HSN), is performing as compared to the overall MSME sector.

Fig.- Jocata Sumpoorn – Industry-wise Sub-Index

Note: A GSTIN is classified as belonging to an industry, if the Trailing Twelve Month (TTM) sales of a particular HSN (at a 2 - digit level) in a month is greater than 50% of the sum of TTM sales of all HSNs by that GSTIN for the same month.

What is HSN?

Harmonized System of Nomenclature is a universally accepted uniform code to classify goods in a systematic way developed by World Customs Organization (WCO) and adopted in our GST regime.

Under the Indian GST system, there are 21 sections comprising 99 chapters divided into 1244 headings and 5224 sub-headings.

For a detailed list of HSNs, visit https://services.gst.gov.in/services/searchhsnsac
